Understanding Gynecomastia and the Need for Surgery

It’s crucial to understand that gynecomastia isn’t solely a result of obesity; hormonal imbalances, genetics, and certain medications can also contribute.

The Gynecomastia Spectrum

Gynecomastia presents itself across a spectrum, ranging from mild to severe cases. However, for those situated at the other end of the spectrum, where the enlargement is significant and resistant to conservative measures, gynecomastia surgery becomes a valuable option.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Navigating the realm of gynecomastia surgery often raises questions, and we’re here to provide answers. Let’s address some commonly asked questions to shed light on this transformative journey.

Is gynecomastia surgery safe?

Gynecomastia surgery, when performed by a skilled and experienced surgeon like Dr. Vishal Purohit, is generally safe. Prior to the surgery, a comprehensive evaluation of your medical history and current health status is conducted to ensure that you are a suitable candidate for the procedure. Will there be visible scars?

Dr. Purohit employs advanced techniques to minimize visible scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas to ensure that scars are as discreet as possible. Over time, these scars tend to fade and become less noticeable, allowing you to enjoy the results of your surgery without the burden of prominent scars.

How long is the recovery period?

Recovery times can vary from patient to patient, depending on factors such as the extent of surgery and individual healing rates. However, most patients can return to light activities within a week after surgery. Engaging in strenuous exercises and heavy lifting should be avoided for a few weeks to allow your body to heal properly and enhance the overall outcome.

Are the results permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally considered permanent. The excess fat and glandular tissue removed during the surgery will not grow back. However, it’s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle to ensure the longevity of your results. Significant weight gain or hormonal changes might impact the outcome over time.

Are there non-surgical alternatives?

While there are non-surgical treatments available for mild cases of gynecomastia, they may not provide the same substantial and lasting results as surgery. For individuals with moderate to severe gynecomastia, surgical intervention, as offered by Dr. Purohit, often provides the most effective solution.
