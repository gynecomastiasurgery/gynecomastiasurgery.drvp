What is Gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia is characterized by the swelling of breast tissue in males, leading to the appearance of larger breasts. This can be caused by a hormonal imbalance, where there’s an increase in estrogen levels relative to testosterone levels. While it’s a benign condition, gynecomastia can lead to physical discomfort and emotional distress, impacting an individual’s self-confidence and self-esteem.

Types of Gynecomastia

There are two primary types of gynecomastia: bilateral and unilateral.

  • Bilateral Gynecomastia: This refers to the enlargement of breast tissue in both breasts. It’s the most common form and can be caused by factors such as hormonal imbalances, obesity, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions.
  • Unilateral Gynecomastia: In this type, only one breast is affected. It can be caused by factors similar to bilateral gynecomastia, including hormonal fluctuations or medical conditions.

3. Will there be noticeable scars?

While some scarring is inevitable, I strategically place incisions to minimize their visibility. Over time, scars tend to fade significantly, becoming less noticeable. My goal is to achieve the best possible aesthetic outcome while minimizing visible scarring.

4. Can exercise alone resolve gynecomastia?

Regular exercise can be beneficial for overall health, but it might not effectively address gynecomastia in all cases. Gynecomastia involves excess glandular tissue and, in some cases, excess fat, which may require surgical intervention for optimal results.

5. Are the results permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are permanent, provided that a healthy lifestyle is maintained. However, it’s important to note that significant weight fluctuations or hormonal imbalances in the future can potentially impact the results.
