Differentiating Between Glandular and Pseudo Gynecomastia

It’s important to understand the underlying causes of gynecomastia to determine the most suitable treatment approach. Glandular gynecomastia involves an actual increase in glandular tissue, resulting in a firmer and more prominent chest. On the other hand, pseudo gynecomastia is characterized by an accumulation of excess fat in the chest area, giving the appearance of enlarged breasts. Distinguishing between these two types of gynecomastia is crucial as it guides us in devising an effective treatment plan.

Understanding Pseudo Gynecomastia

Pseudo gynecomastia, often referred to as “false gynecomastia,” is distinct from glandular gynecomastia as it primarily involves excess adipose tissue rather than glandular growth. This can lead to a softer chest appearance that closely resembles enlarged breasts. While not caused by an increase in glandular tissue, it can still have a significant impact on an individual’s self-esteem and body image. Pseudo gynecomastia is often linked to lifestyle factors such as poor diet, lack of exercise, and hormonal imbalances.

Gynecomastia Surgery with Liposuction

For individuals dealing with pseudo gynecomastia and excess chest fat, gynecomastia surgery with liposuction can be an effective approach. Liposuction is a minimally invasive technique that involves the removal of excess fat deposits through tiny incisions. During the procedure, a thin tube called a cannula is used to gently suction out the fat, resulting in a more sculpted chest contour. This technique not only addresses the physical aspect but also aims to restore self-confidence by achieving a more masculine chest appearance.

Here’s a brief overview of the process of gynecomastia surgery with liposuction:

  1. Consultation: A thorough assessment is conducted to determine if you’re a suitable candidate for the procedure.
  2. Anesthesia: Local or general anesthesia is administered for your comfort during the surgery.
  3. Incision Placement: Small incisions are strategically made in inconspicuous areas to facilitate the insertion of the cannula.
  4. Fat Removal: The cannula is gently moved back and forth to break down and suction out excess fat.
  5. Sculpting: The surgeon skillfully sculpts the chest to achieve a natural and symmetrical appearance.
  6. Recovery: After the procedure, a compression garment may be worn to aid in healing and minimize swelling.
