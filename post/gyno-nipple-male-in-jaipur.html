Q1: What is the recovery time after gynecomastia surgery?

Recovery times can vary based on the extent of the procedure and individual healing rates. Generally, patients can expect to return to light activities within a week or two. Strenuous activities should be avoided for about four to six weeks to allow optimal healing.

Q2: Are the results of surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent, provided that a healthy lifestyle is maintained. Gynecomastia surgery removes excess breast tissue and fat, leading to a flatter chest. However, significant weight gain or hormonal changes can influence the outcome over time.

Q3: Will there be visible scars post-surgery?

Dr. Purohit employs advanced surgical techniques to minimize scarring. Incisions are strategically placed to be as inconspicuous as possible. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable.
