What is the recovery time after surgery?

Recovery times can vary based on individual factors and the extent of the procedure. In general, you can expect an initial recovery period of 1-2 weeks, during which you should avoid strenuous activities. Over the next 2-4 weeks, you can gradually resume light activities. Full recovery, including the ability to engage in more intense exercises, typically occurs within 4-6 weeks post-surgery.

Will there be noticeable scars?

I understand that scarring is a concern for many patients. My surgical techniques aim to minimize scarring as much as possible. Incisions are carefully placed to be discreet and to align with natural body contours. Additionally, I provide guidance on scar management to help minimize their visibility and promote optimal healing.

Are the results permanent?

Gynecomastia surgery can provide long-lasting results. The excess breast tissue and fat that are removed during the procedure typically do not return. However, it’s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle to ensure the longevity of your results. Regular exercise and a balanced diet can contribute to preserving the new chest contour.

Are there any risks associated with the surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery comes with potential risks. These risks can include infection, bleeding, scarring, and changes in sensation around the surgical area. However, I take extensive measures to minimize these risks and ensure your safety.
