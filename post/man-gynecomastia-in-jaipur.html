FAQs: Addressing Common Concerns about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

Navigating the realm of gynecomastia surgery comes with its share of questions and uncertainties. Dr. Vishal Purohit, a seasoned MCh Plastic Surgeon in Jaipur, understands the importance of addressing these concerns. Here are some commonly asked questions about gynecomastia surgery and his expert insights to guide you.

1. Is gynecomastia surgery painful? Gynecomastia surgery is performed under anesthesia, ensuring a pain-free experience during the procedure itself. Post-surgery, mild discomfort is normal and can be managed with prescribed pain medications. Rest assured, Dr. Purohit’s techniques prioritize patient comfort and aim for a smooth recovery process.

2. How long is the recovery period? Recovery timelines can vary based on individual factors and the extent of the procedure. Generally, patients can expect to resume light activities within a week and gradually return to regular routines within a few weeks. Dr. Purohit provides comprehensive postoperative instructions to support a swift and safe recovery.

3. Are the results permanent? Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are typically permanent. The removal of excess fat and glandular tissue leads to a more defined and masculine chest contour. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle through balanced diet and regular exercise is essential to sustain the achieved results over time.

4. Will there be noticeable scars? Dr. Purohit employs meticulous techniques to minimize scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas, and over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. Following postoperative care instructions and avoiding sun exposure on incision sites can further contribute to scar fading.

5. Can I resume physical activities after surgery? Gradually resuming physical activities is recommended, but it’s essential to follow Dr. Purohit’s guidelines. Strenuous exercises should be avoided during the initial weeks to prevent complications. As healing progresses, patients can incrementally reintegrate exercise into their routines, ensuring a safe and effective recovery.
