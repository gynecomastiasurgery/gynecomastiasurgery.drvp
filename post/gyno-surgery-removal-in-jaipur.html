FAQs About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As a seasoned Mch Plastic Surgeon in Jaipur, I understand that gynecomastia surgery raises a multitude of questions. In this section, I address some of the most commonly asked questions to provide you with a clearer understanding of the procedure and its outcomes.

**1. Who is an ideal candidate for gynecomastia surgery?

Gynecomastia surgery is suitable for individuals who have excessive glandular tissue, fat deposits, or both, leading to an enlarged male chest. Ideal candidates are those who have tried non-surgical methods without satisfactory results. However, a comprehensive consultation is essential to determine candidacy, as medical history, overall health, and individual goals play a crucial role.

**2. What is the recovery period like?

Recovery after gynecomastia surgery typically involves a few days of rest and limited physical activity. Compression garments are worn to aid in swelling reduction and support the healing process. While individual recovery times may vary, most patients can resume light activities within a week and gradually return to more strenuous activities over a few weeks.

**3. Are the results permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent. Once glandular tissue and excess fat are removed, they do not grow back. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is crucial to prevent the recurrence of fat deposits. Significant weight fluctuations or the use of certain medications may influence long-term results.

**4. Will there be visible scarring?

Gynecomastia surgery incisions are strategically placed to minimize visibility. Incisions are often made in inconspicuous areas, such as around the areola or within natural skin folds. While scars are inevitable, they tend to fade over time and become less noticeable with proper scar care and protection from sun exposure.

**5. How do I choose between surgical and non-surgical options?

The choice between surgical and non-surgical options depends on the severity of your gynecomastia and your desired outcomes. Non-surgical treatments are ideal for individuals with mild cases or those seeking less invasive approaches. Surgical options provide more comprehensive results for moderate to severe cases. During our consultation, we will discuss your goals and recommend the most suitable approach based on your individual needs.
