FAQs: Answers to Commonly Asked Questions About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

1. What is the recovery time after gynecomastia surgery?

The recovery period following gynecomastia surgery can vary depending on individual factors and the extent of the procedure. Generally, patients can expect mild discomfort, swelling, and bruising in the initial days. You might need to wear a compression garment to aid in healing and support the chest’s new contours. Dr. Purohit will provide specific post-operative care instructions tailored to your needs, helping you navigate the recovery process with confidence.

2. Are the results of the procedure permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery can be permanent, especially when performed by a skilled surgeon like Dr. Vishal Purohit. The removal of excess glandular tissue and fat provides lasting changes to the chest’s appearance. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including proper diet and regular exercise, can contribute to the longevity of your results.

3. Can exercise alone resolve gynecomastia?

In cases of true gynecomastia, where glandular tissue is enlarged, exercise alone might not be sufficient to eliminate the issue. Gynecomastia is often caused by hormonal imbalances or genetic factors that can’t be addressed solely through exercise. Male chest fat reduction surgery, performed by Dr. Purohit, targets the underlying causes to achieve a more masculine chest contour.

4. Is gynecomastia surgery suitable for all body types?

Gynecomastia surgery is suitable for a wide range of body types, but a personalized consultation with Dr. Purohit is necessary to determine the best approach for your specific case. Whether you have mild or severe gynecomastia, Dr. Purohit’s expertise enables him to tailor the procedure to your unique needs, ensuring the best possible outcomes.

5. Are there any potential risks associated with the procedure?

As with any surgical procedure, there are inherent risks involved in gynecomastia surgery. However, under the skilled hands of Dr. Purohit, complications are rare.
