The Surgical Process

On the day of your surgery, you’ll be under the care of experienced medical professionals. The procedure generally follows these steps:

  1. Anesthesia: You’ll receive anesthesia to ensure your comfort during the surgery. The type of anesthesia used will be discussed and decided during your consultation.
  2. Incision Placement: Depending on the extent of your gynecomastia and your individual anatomy, incisions will be strategically placed. Common incision patterns include around the areola or in inconspicuous areas.
  3. Tissue Removal: Excess breast tissue and fat are meticulously removed through the incisions. This step involves a combination of surgical techniques to ensure optimal results.
  4. Chest Contouring: Once the excess tissue is removed, the chest is contoured to achieve a natural and masculine appearance. Attention to detail is crucial during this phase to ensure symmetry and proportion.
  5. Incision Closure: The incisions are carefully closed using sutures. In some cases, drainage tubes may be placed to prevent fluid accumulation.

Recovery and Results

Recovery after gynecomastia surgery varies from person to person, but typically, you’ll be advised to rest and avoid strenuous activities for a certain period. You may experience swelling and discomfort initially, which is a normal part of the healing process. Following post-operative instructions diligently is essential for a smooth recovery and optimal results.
