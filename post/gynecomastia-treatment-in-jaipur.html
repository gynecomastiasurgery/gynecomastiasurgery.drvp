Prevalence and Significance

Enlarged male breast tissue, a condition characterized by the abnormal growth of glandular breast tissue in men, is more common than one might think. Studies suggest that up to 65% of adolescent boys and a substantial portion of adult men experience gynecomastia at some point in their lives1. While not a life-threatening condition, gynecomastia can have profound psychological implications, often leading to self-consciousness and reluctance to participate in activities that require exposing the chest.

Causes and Contributing Factors

Gynecomastia can be triggered by a variety of factors, both physiological and hormonal. Hormonal imbalances between estrogen and testosterone levels can play a pivotal role in its development. Other contributing factors include obesity, certain medications, alcohol and drug use, and underlying medical conditions2. Understanding the root cause is essential for devising an effective treatment plan, a task at which Dr. Vishal Purohit excels.
