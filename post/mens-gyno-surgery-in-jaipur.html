Unveiling the Challenge

  1. Hormonal Whirlwind: Hormones play a crucial role in our bodies, and sometimes, they can stir up unexpected changes. An imbalance in estrogen and testosterone levels can trigger the development of breast tissue, leading to the appearance of one-sided gynecomastia.   2. Genetic Predisposition: Genetics can be a double-edged sword. While they shape our features and characteristics, they can also influence the likelihood of developing gynecomastia. If there’s a family history of this condition, the chances of experiencing it, particularly in a one-sided manner, can increase.

FAQs about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

1. What causes gynecomastia in men?

Gynecomastia is often triggered by an imbalance in hormones, specifically an increase in estrogen relative to testosterone. This hormonal shift can result from various factors such as puberty, aging, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions. During your consultation, Dr. Vishal Purohit will delve into your medical history to identify potential causes and tailor a suitable treatment plan.

2. How do I know if I have one-sided gynecomastia?

One-sided gynecomastia is characterized by uneven enlargement of the male breast tissue, predominantly affecting one side. You may notice a visible imbalance in your chest appearance or feel tenderness in one breast. Dr. Purohit’s expertise allows him to diagnose the condition accurately during your consultation and recommend the most suitable approach for your specific case.
