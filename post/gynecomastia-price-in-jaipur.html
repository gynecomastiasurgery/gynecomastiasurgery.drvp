Q2: Is gynecomastia surgery a safe procedure?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery is generally safe when performed by a qualified and experienced plastic surgeon. I prioritize patient safety and adhere to rigorous standards and protocols. Q3: How long does the recovery take?

Recovery duration can vary depending on the extent of the procedure and individual healing factors. Most patients can expect to resume light activities within about a week after surgery. More strenuous activities and exercise might be gradually reintroduced within a few weeks. I provide detailed post-operative instructions to guide you through a smooth recovery process.

Q4: Will there be noticeable scars?

Efforts are made to minimize scarring during gynecomastia surgery. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas to reduce visibility. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. Following proper wound care and scar management recommendations can further promote optimal healing.

Q5: Can exercise eliminate gynecomastia?

Exercise can help improve overall body composition and muscle tone, but it might not be sufficient to eliminate gynecomastia caused by glandular tissue enlargement. Glandular tissue doesn’t respond well to exercise or diet. Surgical intervention, such as gynecomastia surgery, is often the most effective solution for removing excess tissue and achieving the desired chest contour.
