FAQs: Your Gynecomastia Surgery Queries Answered

Question 1: What’s the recovery time after gynecomastia surgery?

The recovery timeline following gynecomastia surgery varies based on individual factors and the extent of the procedure. Generally, patients can expect to return to work and normal activities within 1 to 2 weeks. However, strenuous exercises should be avoided for about 4 to 6 weeks to ensure optimal healing. Dr. Vishal Purohit and his team provide comprehensive post-operative instructions to guide you through a smooth and effective recovery.

Question 2: How long do the results of the surgery last?

The results of gynecomastia surgery are considered permanent, provided a healthy lifestyle is maintained. Significant weight fluctuations or hormonal changes might impact the results over time. Dr. Purohit emphasizes the importance of maintaining a stable weight and adhering to a healthy regimen to prolong the positive outcomes of the surgery.

Question 3: Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

Most patients report mild discomfort rather than severe pain after gynecomastia surgery. Any discomfort can be managed with prescribed pain medications and adhering to post-operative care instructions. Dr. Purohit’s expertise ensures that the surgical process is as minimally invasive as possible, contributing to a smoother recovery experience.

Question 4: Can exercise alone eliminate gynecomastia?

In cases of mild gynecomastia, targeted exercises may help improve chest appearance to a certain extent. However, for cases where excess glandular tissue or fat is present, surgical intervention is often the most effective solution. Dr. Vishal Purohit’s gynecomastia excision techniques offer a comprehensive and lasting solution for addressing such concerns.

Question 5: Are there any age restrictions for the surgery?

Gynecomastia surgery can be performed on individuals of various age groups, provided they are in good health. It’s important to note that hormonal changes during adolescence can sometimes lead to temporary gynecomastia, which might resolve on its own. Dr. Purohit evaluates each patient’s unique situation to determine the most suitable course of action.
