The Procedure: Precision in Action

The surgery typically involves two key steps: excision of glandular tissue and liposuction. Glandular tissue excision is essential for cases where excess breast tissue is present. During this step, a small incision is made around the areola (the dark area around the nipple), allowing for the precise removal of glandular tissue. This step not only reduces the physical volume but also contributes to a flatter, more sculpted chest contour.

Liposuction, on the other hand, addresses fatty deposits that might be contributing to the appearance of gynecomastia. A thin, hollow tube (cannula) is inserted through small incisions, and excess fat is gently suctioned out. This technique ensures smoother transitions and enhances the overall chest definition. The combination of glandular tissue excision and liposuction allows for a comprehensive and balanced transformation.
