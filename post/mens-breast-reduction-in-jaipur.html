Leading a healthy lifestyle that includes regular exercise and a balanced diet can contribute to reducing gynecomastia, especially in cases where the condition is mild. In some cases, medical interventions can help manage gynecomastia. These interventions may involve medications that target hormonal imbalances contributing to the condition. It’s crucial to consult with a medical professional before considering any medication, as they can provide guidance on the appropriateness and potential side effects.

3. Compression Garments

Compression garments, often used by athletes, can help temporarily conceal the appearance of gynecomastia by providing support and minimizing the appearance of the chest. While this doesn’t address the underlying cause, it can offer a short-term solution for individuals seeking to manage the condition without surgery.
